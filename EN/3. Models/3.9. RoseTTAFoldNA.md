# 3.9. RoseTTAFoldNA

[[Home|Home]] > [[EN/3. Models/3.0. Models Overview|Models]] > RoseTTAFoldNA
🇺🇦 [[UA/3. Моделі/3.9. RoseTTAFoldNA|Українська]]

`RoseTTAFoldNA` extends the `RoseTTAFold` family from proteins toward nucleic acids and `protein–DNA` / `protein–RNA` complexes. It is one of the most useful focused references when a knowledge base wants stronger coverage of nucleic-acid-aware structure prediction.

## Why RoseTTAFoldNA matters

Protein-only structure prediction improved much earlier than direct prediction of nucleic-acid complexes.
That left a practical gap:

- protein monomer prediction became strong;
- docking-style assembly of nucleic acid complexes remained hard;
- `protein–DNA` and `protein–RNA` interfaces were still a weak spot.

RoseTTAFoldNA matters because it targets that gap directly.

## Main idea

The model keeps the three-track spirit of `RoseTTAFold`, but extends all major representations to include nucleic acids:

- extra 1D tokens for DNA and RNA residues;
- generalized 2D pair features for `protein–NA` and `NA–NA` interactions;
- generalized 3D coordinate reasoning for nucleotide geometry.

## Strengths

| Strength | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Direct protein–NA focus | Better conceptual fit than protein-only baselines |
| Confidence estimates | Useful for filtering plausible interface predictions |
| Good complement to AF3 | Helps compare generalist vs specialized reasoning |

## When RoseTTAFoldNA is useful

- when a task is mainly about `protein–DNA` or `protein–RNA` complexes;
- when a specialized nucleic-acid-aware baseline is needed;
- when the goal is to understand whether failure comes from general complex modeling or from NA-specific geometry.

## Limitations

| Limitation | Why it matters |
|---|---|
| Narrower than AF3 | It is specialized, not a single universal biomolecular model |
| Less suited for ligand-heavy tasks | Its core value is nucleic-acid interaction modeling |
| Hard cases remain hard | Large flexible nucleic acids and unusual interfaces still challenge prediction |
