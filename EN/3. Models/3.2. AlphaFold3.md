# 3.2. AlphaFold3

[[Home|Home]] > [[EN/3. Models/3.0. Models Overview|Models]] > AlphaFold3
🇺🇦 [[UA/3. Моделі/3.2. AlphaFold3|Українська]]

`AlphaFold3` is a general model for biomolecular complex structure prediction across proteins, RNA, DNA, ligands, ions, and modified residues within a single framework.

## Why AlphaFold3 became possible and why it matters

After `AlphaFold2`, it became clear that deep learning could model protein geometry extremely well.
The next step was not only to improve monomer prediction, but to move toward a broader biomolecular space:

- protein-protein interfaces;
- protein-ligand interactions;
- protein-DNA / protein-RNA complexes;
- systems with modifications, cofactors, and ions.

AF3 matters because it treats these cases as variants of one joint 3D modeling problem rather than as a collection of completely separate tools.

## Architectural idea

At a high level, AF3 combines:

- an `input representation` for multiple entity types;
- `Pairformer` as the trunk for pair- and token-level updates;
- a `diffusion-based structure module` for coordinate generation;
- confidence heads for ranking and quality estimation.

## What separates AF3 from its predecessors

| Aspect | AlphaFold2 | AlphaFold3 |
|---|---|---|
| Main focus | Mostly proteins | General biomolecular complexes |
| Central trunk | Evoformer | Pairformer |
| Structure generation | Structure module | Diffusion-based structure generation |
| Entity coverage | Mostly polymer-centric | Proteins + nucleic acids + ligands + ions + modifications |
| Practical effect | Revolution in protein folding | Attempt at broader unified biomolecular modeling |

## Properties

- **Multimolecular coverage**: one model works across multiple biomolecular entity types.
- **Unified geometric reasoning**: the complex is modeled jointly rather than as independently solved parts.
- **Stronger ligand / nucleic-acid coverage**: one of the main advances over AF2.
- **Diffusion-based generation**: coordinates are generated through iterative refinement rather than simple direct regression.
- **Confidence-aware ranking**: the model estimates not only structure but also prediction reliability.

## Best-use scenarios

- when a system contains proteins together with DNA/RNA and small molecules;
- when the full complex matters more than a single isolated chain;
- when a unified baseline is preferable to separate docking and structure tools;
- when biological interpretation depends on multi-entity interactions.

## Limitations

- **High computational cost**: a generalist model of this scope is resource-intensive.
- **Not a replacement for experiment**: even strong predictions still require validation, especially in chemistry-sensitive cases.
- **Different regimes fail differently**: ligand docking, antibody binding, and large assemblies each have distinct failure modes.
- **Limited conformational diversity**: one static prediction may not capture the real ensemble of states.
- **Confidence is not ground truth**: a high internal score is not a substitute for independent assessment.

## Practical context

AF3 is best viewed not as a final solver for every structural problem, but as a strong generalist model for a broad class of biomolecular interaction tasks.
In many workflows it is most useful together with:

- separate stereochemical validation;
- docking or MD tools for refinement;
- biological inspection of interface plausibility and binding modes.
