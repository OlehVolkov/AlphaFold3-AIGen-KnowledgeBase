# 3.6. OpenFold

[[Home|Home]] > [[EN/3. Models/3.0. Models Overview|Models]] > OpenFold
🇺🇦 [[UA/3. Моделі/3.6. OpenFold|Українська]]

`OpenFold` is a trainable, memory-efficient PyTorch reproduction of `AlphaFold2`. It matters less as a new benchmark winner and more as the main open engineering reference for training, modifying, and analyzing `AF2`-class systems.

## Why OpenFold matters

The original `AlphaFold2` release transformed structure prediction, but practical retraining remained difficult because the full training stack and data plumbing were not packaged as a community-friendly system.

OpenFold matters because it closes that gap:

- it reproduces `AF2` behavior in a trainable open implementation;
- it makes ablation, retraining, and architecture modification much easier;
- it is a natural bridge from pure `AF2` replication toward newer open systems such as `Boltz-1`.

## Main idea

OpenFold keeps the core `AF2` recipe but emphasizes reproducibility and engineering access:

- Evoformer-style protein reasoning;
- structure-module prediction in the `AF2` family;
- training from scratch on open structural datasets;
- a PyTorch codebase rather than a closed research implementation.

## Strengths

| Strength | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Trainable | Useful for research on scaling, new losses, and new modalities |
| Reproducible | Makes `AF2`-class results easier to audit and extend |
| Engineering-friendly | Better suited for local experimentation than a black-box server |
| Strong baseline | Good reference point for any open protein-structure pipeline |

## Where OpenFold is especially useful

- when the goal is **method development**, not just inference;
- when a lab needs to inspect or modify an `AF2`-class architecture;
- when new tasks such as `protein-ligand` or custom confidence heads are being prototyped;
- when reproducible training behavior is as important as final prediction quality.

## Limitations

| Limitation | Why it matters |
| --- | --- |
| Still an `AF2`-family system | It is not a full `AF3`-style general multimolecular model |
| Heavy training stack | Retraining remains expensive even in an open implementation |
| Proteins-first orientation | It is mainly the best open reference for protein structure work |
