## Confidence Metrics

### pLDDT (predicted Local Distance Difference Test)
- Predicts structure quality at the **per-atom** level
- Scale: 0–100 (higher is better)
- Colour coding in visualizations:
  - 🟠 0–50: very low confidence
  - 🟡 50–70: low
  - 🔵 70–90: high
  - 💙 90–100: very high

### PAE (Predicted Aligned Error)
- Pairwise error matrix between **chains / domains**
- Reflects confidence in **relative positions** of segments
- Darker colour = smaller error = higher confidence

### ipTM (interface predicted TM-score)
- Estimates confidence at the **interface** between two chains
- Strongly correlates with actual accuracy (DockQ, iLDDT)

### PDE (Predicted Distance Error)
- Error of the predicted distance matrix relative to the true structure

## Calibration

AF3 confidence metrics are **well-calibrated** with actual accuracy:
- Higher ipTM → higher DockQ for protein–protein interfaces
- Higher pLDDT → higher LDDT_to_polymer for proteins, nucleic acids, ligands

## Practical Use

- **Identifying disordered regions:** low pLDDT signals structural disorder
- **Assessing complex quality:** PAE shows whether domains are positioned correctly relative to each other
- **Ranking predictions:** a combination of pTM + ipTM + penalties for clashes and disorder
