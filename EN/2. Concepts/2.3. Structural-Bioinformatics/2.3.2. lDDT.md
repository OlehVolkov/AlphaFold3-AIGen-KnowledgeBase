# lDDT — Local Distance Difference Test

[[Home|Home]] > [[EN/Index|Concepts]] > Structural Bioinformatics
🇺🇦 [[UA/2. Концепції/2.3. Структурна-Біоінформатика/2.3.2. lDDT|Українська]]

> **lDDT** is a local superposition-free metric that measures how well a model preserves local interatomic distances relative to a reference structure.

## Why lDDT is convenient

Global metrics such as RMSD are useful for average shape, but they can be unstable under:

- domain motions;
- flexible loops;
- local errors inside an otherwise good structure.

lDDT is useful precisely because it does not require global superposition and instead focuses on local geometric neighborhoods.

## Formal definition

For atom pairs that lie within a local inclusion radius in the reference structure, one checks how accurately distances are preserved.

In simplified form:

$$\mathrm{lDDT} = \frac{1}{4}\sum_{t\in\{0.5,1,2,4\}\text{ \AA}} \frac{\#\text{preserved contacts at }t}{\#\text{all reference contacts}}$$

where a contact is preserved if:

$$\left|d_{ij}^{\mathrm{pred}}-d_{ij}^{\mathrm{ref}}\right|\le t$$

and the pair belongs to the local reference neighborhood.

## Intuition

Instead of asking "how close are the two structures after alignment?", lDDT asks:

"is the local geometric environment around this atom or residue preserved?"

That makes it especially useful for:

- local defects;
- multi-domain proteins;
- complexes where global superposition is not always informative.

## pLDDT in AlphaFold-like models

`pLDDT` (`predicted lDDT`) is not measured lDDT, but the model's own predicted local confidence.

$$\mathrm{pLDDT}_i \in [0,100]$$

So pLDDT answers:

"how likely does the model think this local geometry is to be correct relative to an unknown experimental structure?"

## Typical pLDDT interpretation

| pLDDT | Interpretation |
|---|---|
| `> 90` | Very high local confidence |
| `70–90` | Generally reliable model region |
| `50–70` | Possible flexibility or local uncertainty |
| `< 50` | Very low confidence, possible disorder |

## Strengths of lDDT

- **No global superposition required**.
- **Better local-quality signal** than a single global metric.
- **Useful for per-residue confidence reasoning**.
- **More robust to domain shifts** than RMSD.

## Limitations of lDDT

- **Says less about global topology** than RMSD or TM-score.
- **Depends on the local neighborhood definition and thresholds**.
- **May not strongly penalize large global rearrangements** if local neighborhoods remain partly preserved.
